AI won't fix
your marketing.

AI & Strategy·5 min read·Hamed Ashouri

Everyone is racing to add AI to their marketing. Almost no one is racing to add clarity. And that's the problem — because AI doesn't fix unclear marketing. It scales it.

Here's the uncomfortable truth nobody selling an AI tool will tell you: AI makes bad marketing faster, not better. Point a powerful model at a vague strategy and you don't get clarity — you get vagueness, beautifully formatted, at volume. More posts that say nothing. More emails nobody opens. More "content," less meaning.

AI is a multiplier, not a strategy

Think of AI as a multiplier sitting in the middle of your marketing:

  • Feed it a clear strategy — a sharp position, one audience, one goal — and it becomes a genuine superpower. It drafts, structures, and scales the right message.
  • Feed it confusion — no position, no audience, "we help everyone" — and it multiplies the confusion. Now you're unclear in ten formats instead of one.

The output is only ever as good as the thinking you put in. AI raises the ceiling on execution. It does nothing for the foundation.

A multiplier amplifies whatever you give it. Multiply clarity and you win. Multiply noise and you just get louder noise.

Why "AI-first" backfires

When teams lead with the tool instead of the strategy, three things happen. They produce more — and feel productive. The metrics that measure activity go up. And the metric that matters — qualified inquiries — doesn't move. Because the audience still can't tell who you're for or why you. You've automated the wrong thing.

This is the difference between an AI tool and a consultancy. A tool gives you output. Judgment decides whether that output is true to your business — and that part is still, stubbornly, human.

The order that works

Get the sequence right and AI earns its reputation:

  • 1. Clarity first. Decide who you serve, what you stand for, and the one goal for the next 90 days.
  • 2. Strategy second. Turn that into a position, content pillars, and a simple funnel.
  • 3. Then automate. Now let AI draft, repurpose, and scale — because it finally has something true to amplify.

That order is everything. Skip the first two steps and the third just helps you fail faster.

The takeaway

AI is the best execution partner marketing has ever had. It is a terrible substitute for a decision. Use it to move faster after you're clear — never as a way to avoid getting clear. Fix the strategy, then hand the robots something worth multiplying.
