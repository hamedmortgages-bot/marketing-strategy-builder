The business &
marketing briefing.

Briefing·July 1, 2026·Hamed Ashouri

A curated snapshot of what's actually moving across marketing, advertising, AI and the wider economy — the signal, not the noise. Here's what's worth a business owner's attention this week.

Marketing & advertising

  • Google is rebuilding its ad stack around Gemini and relabeling Smart Bidding to separate "volume" from "efficiency" goals; advertisers have until Feb 2027 to migrate legacy campaigns. — Impression, ALM Corp
  • Meta launched AI connectors that let advertisers run Meta ads from external AI tools, and will use third-party site activity to personalise feeds and ads. — Boot Camp Digital
  • Google's share of US search-ad spend is projected to fall below 50% by year-end — paid budgets are fragmenting across TikTok and retail media. — Two Octobers

AI for marketing

  • 87% of marketers now use generative AI in a workflow — up from 51% in 2024 — with AI-assisted campaigns reporting roughly 22% higher ROI. — Salesforce, State of Marketing 2026
  • The frontier is shifting from content generation to agentic execution: autonomous assistants that prospect, qualify and book meetings. — MarTech
  • New tools now audit brand voice and compliance inside the content workflow, before anything ships. — MarTech

Social & content trends

  • Instagram cut hashtags to 5 per post (from 30) — keywords now drive discovery — while Reels can run up to 20 minutes. — StoryChief, Sprout Social
  • Substack has become a full social platform (feed, DMs, profiles), and LinkedIn newsletters build audience depth ordinary posts can't. — Hootsuite
  • Short-form video still delivers the best ROI for 31% of marketers, but long-form is quietly rebounding as audiences seek depth. — Hootsuite

Business & economy

  • Ontario's small-business tax rate drops from 3.2% to 2.2%, effective today — relief for more than 375,000 small businesses. — Ontario Budget
  • Deloitte projects Canada's economy rebounding late-2026 (0.7% growth this year); "insufficient demand" is now firms' top constraint — a direct opening for marketing that creates demand. — BNN Bloomberg
One move to make this weekTake one strong insight and publish it three ways — a LinkedIn carousel, a short-form video, and a newsletter note. In a soft-demand economy, the businesses that keep showing up with a clear message are the ones that get remembered when budgets loosen.

Sources: Two Octobers, Impression, ALM Corp, Boot Camp Digital, MarTech, Salesforce (State of Marketing 2026), Hootsuite, Sprout Social, StoryChief, BNN Bloomberg. Compiled July 1, 2026.
